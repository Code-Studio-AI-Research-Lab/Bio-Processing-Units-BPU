# 🌿 Onboarding Guide: Literature Review & Research Matrix for Biodegradable Computing

Welcome to the **Code Studio AI Research Lab**. This guide establishes the mandatory workflow, literature logging standards, and Git guidelines for all team members working on our project: **"Biodegradable Digital Logic & Computational Matter"**.

---

## 🔍 1. Where to Find High-Impact Papers
Synthetic biology and bio-computing are highly rigorous, elite niches. Search across molecular biology, chemistry, and advanced computer systems using:
* **Google Scholar:** [scholar.google.com](https://scholar.google.com/) (Filter: *Since 2024/2025* for cutting-edge protein folding simulations and DNA computation matrixes).
* **Premier Journals:** *Nature Biotechnology*, *Science*, *ACS Synthetic Biology*, and *IEEE Transactions on Nanobioscience*.
* **Top AI/Systems Conferences:** **NeurIPS (Computational Biology tracks)**, **ISMB** (Intelligent Systems for Molecular Biology).

---

## 🛠️ 2. Search Strategy & Keywords
Avoid common environmental blogs; search using exact computational biochemistry syntax:
* **Query 1:** `"DNA computing" AND "Logic gates" AND "Error correction"`
* **Query 2:** `"Synthetic biology circuits" AND "Enzyme kinetics" AND "Boolean logic"`
* **Query 3:** `"Biodegradable electronics" AND "Biopolymers" AND "Molecular simulation"`

---

## 📖 3. How to Read & Critically Evaluate a Bio-Computing Paper
1. **The Processing Architecture:** Did the authors achieve true Turing-complete processing (capable of executing loops and decisions), or did they just build a simple one-off gate?
2. **Signal Degradation:** Analyze their degradation logs. Did the biological circuit rot away too quickly while doing a task, or did it stay active as long as the data needed to process?
3. **The Practical Gap (Our Opportunity):** Look closely at their **Limitations**. Most papers focus on pure test-tube laboratory chemistry. *Our core gap is building a scalable computational simulation model that lets researchers design BPUs on a computer before synthesizing them in wet-labs.*
